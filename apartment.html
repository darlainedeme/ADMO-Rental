What it's like

The space, in plain words.

It's unit 4 in a three-story brick condo from 1900. Just four apartments in the whole building, intercom entry, cameras at the door, and neighbors who actually say hi in the stairwell. That bit matters more than people realize: this isn't a 200-unit tower where you ride the elevator next to strangers. You'll know who lives upstairs by the second week.

Inside, the kitchen and living area share one open room, and the kitchen is genuinely good — stainless appliances, built-in microwave, dishwasher, garbage disposal, upgraded counters. Hardwood floors throughout, recessed lighting, and a spa-feel bathroom with a double vanity and serious tile work. At 599 sq ft (about 56 m²) it's not huge, but the layout works and nothing feels tight.

Central A/C keeps it comfortable through DC summers, forced-air heat through the winters, and the washer-dryer stack in the unit means you never see a laundromat. Gas, water, and trash are covered by the condo fee.

You won't need a car. The 42 bus runs straight downtown from the corner, three Capital Bikeshare stations sit within a block, and the walk score is 99. If you'd like, I'll leave the bike too — it's the fastest way around the neighborhood and gets you to Rock Creek trails in about five minutes.

The bedroom faces the back, so weekend energy on 18th Street stays outside. You'll hear the neighborhood on Friday and Saturday nights, but not through the walls.
